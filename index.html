02Architecture

Three diagrams — topology, multi-link failover, and QoS traffic prioritization — explained step by step.

Spoke-Hub Topology

  1. One control point, not thousands of peers. Every branch edge tunnels back to one place — the data center hub — instead of meshing directly with every other branch.
  2. vManage sits at the hub as the control plane. It pushes policy — routing, QoS, security — so a rule change is made once centrally, not 4,000 times manually.
  3. Each spoke carries its own physical links underneath — that's what the next diagram breaks down.

Multi-Link Failover Order

active link
failover trigger
standby link
  1. MPLS is primary — carries traffic under normal conditions because it's the most stable, lowest-latency path.
  2. ILL is backup — leased-line fallback if MPLS goes down.
  3. 4G takes over next — wireless, higher latency, but keeps critical traffic moving.
  4. PPPoE is the next fallback — broadband/DSL when the wired backup is gone.
  5. RF is the last resort — brought in only when every wired and cellular option has failed.

QoS — Application-Aware Traffic Prioritization

  1. All traffic hits the QoS engine first, where it's classified by application — not just by port or protocol.
  2. Voice and video go to the high-priority queue — most sensitive to delay and jitter, so protected first.
  3. Core business apps sit in a medium queue — protected from congestion, behind real-time traffic.
  4. Everything else drops to best-effort — bulk transfers absorb congestion so critical traffic doesn't suffer.
